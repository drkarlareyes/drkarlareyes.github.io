**Published in:** *New Technology in Education and Training* (Springer).

This chapter presents a multi-disciplinary case study on the integration of research-based methodologies into engineering curricula. Drawing from experiences in teaching advanced biomedical and mechatronic engineering concepts, the text outlines how to transition students from passive learners to active researchers.

We discuss the pedagogical frameworks required to teach complex subjects—such as medical device regulations (ISO 13485) and biomechanics—through hands-on, project-based learning. The chapter serves as a guide for academic institutions aiming to foster innovation, critical thinking, and applied engineering skills in their students.
