**Published in:** *Biomedical Engineering Systems and Technologies* (Springer).

This book chapter details the validation of the SiViS dataset, focusing on the complex challenges of multi-patient monitoring using radar technology. It provides an in-depth analysis of how physical radar placement and spatial configuration affect the accuracy of heart and respiration rate estimations. 

The chapter discusses the physical simulation of the human thorax and how the Radar Cross Section (RCS) is impacted by multi-path interference when multiple subjects are present in the same monitored environment. This work serves as a foundational text for researchers building edge-case deep learning models for non-contact clinical monitoring.
