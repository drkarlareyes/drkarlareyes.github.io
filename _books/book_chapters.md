**Published in:** *Biomedical Engineering Systems and Technologies* (Springer).

This book chapter details the validation of the SiViS dataset, focusing on the complex challenges of multi-patient monitoring using radar technology. It provides an in-depth analysis of how physical radar placement and spatial configuration affect the accuracy of heart and respiration rate estimations. 

The chapter discusses the physical simulation of the human thorax and how the Radar Cross Section (RCS) is impacted by multi-path interference when multiple subjects are present in the same monitored environment. This work serves as a foundational text for researchers building edge-case deep learning models for non-contact clinical monitoring.

[cite_start]**Published in:** *Advances in Digital Health and Medical Bioengineering* (Springer)[cite: 239].

This chapter explores the systemic integration of digital health technologies within developing healthcare infrastructures. We assess the adoption rates, technical barriers, and workflow challenges associated with implementing Electronic Health Records (EHR) and Health Information Systems (HIS) in Honduran hospitals.

The research highlights the critical gap between technological availability and clinical execution, offering a framework for addressing data harmonization, user-interface accessibility, and infrastructure limitations. It provides a strategic roadmap for policy-makers and clinical data engineers aiming to deploy scalable digital health solutions in resource-constrained environments.

**Published in:** *New Technology in Education and Training* (Springer).

This chapter presents a multi-disciplinary case study on the integration of research-based methodologies into engineering curricula. Drawing from experiences in teaching advanced biomedical and mechatronic engineering concepts, the text outlines how to transition students from passive learners to active researchers.

We discuss the pedagogical frameworks required to teach complex subjects—such as medical device regulations (ISO 13485) and biomechanics—through hands-on, project-based learning. The chapter serves as a guide for academic institutions aiming to foster innovation, critical thinking, and applied engineering skills in their students.
